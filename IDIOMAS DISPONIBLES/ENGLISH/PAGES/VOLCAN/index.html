The Misfortune of the Canarians


What has happened to our fellows?

The recent volcanic eruption in La Palma, in the Canary Islands, has generated deep concern and mobilization both locally and internationally. The eruption, which began on May 16th, has triggered lava flows and volcanic gas emissions, forcing the evacuation of thousands of residents and causing significant damage to infrastructure and properties. Local authorities and emergency response teams have been working tirelessly to manage the situation and ensure the safety of those affected. Temporary shelters have been established, and civil protection measures have been implemented to mitigate risks and provide support to displaced people.



Challenges They Face

The volcanic eruption in La Palma has generated a series of urgent problems. Evacuation of residents, damage to infrastructure, and disrupted basic services are key concerns. Additionally, volcanic activity poses risks to health and the environment, and affects economic sectors such as tourism and agriculture. Recovery will require a coordinated response to effectively address humanitarian, environmental, and economic impacts.



What Can We Do to Help?


Support Them Through Donations

Supporting the Canary people in this time of crisis is crucial to helping the communities affected by the volcanic eruption in La Palma. Your generosity can make a difference by providing urgent humanitarian aid, such as food, water, and shelter, to the thousands of displaced residents. Additionally, your donation will contribute to rebuilding damaged infrastructure and the economic recovery of the region, helping restore stability and the well-being of Canarian families. Join us in this solidarity effort and show our support for the Canary people in their time of need. Every contribution, no matter how small, counts and makes a real difference in the lives of those who need it most.

Support Them Through Demonstrations

Your support for demonstrations in solidarity with the Canary people is crucial at this time of need. By joining the protests, you are raising your voice to demand attention and action in response to the crisis caused by the volcanic eruption in La Palma. Your presence demonstrates solidarity with the affected communities and puts pressure on authorities to provide necessary support in terms of humanitarian aid, reconstruction, and economic recovery. Together, we can send a powerful message of unity and solidarity, showing the Canary people that they are not alone in their struggle to rebuild and recover. Join us in the demonstrations and let our voices be heard in support of the Canary people.
