Water Scarcity in Catalonia


Current Situation

Drought in Catalonia is wreaking havoc on the agricultural sector, threatening crops, productivity, and livelihoods of farmers. Additionally, water scarcity affects food security, employment, and the local economy, increasing pressure on natural resources and exacerbating negative effects on biodiversity and aquatic ecosystems. Urgent actions are needed to effectively manage water, promote sustainable agricultural practices, and ensure a secure supply to protect farmers and vulnerable communities in Catalonia.



Challenges They Face

Drought leads to a decrease in river and reservoir flow, affecting the supply of drinking water for domestic, industrial, and agricultural use. This situation can lead to water consumption restrictions, rationing, and increased production costs. Environmentally, drought also has negative consequences such as loss of biodiversity, degradation of aquatic ecosystems, and proliferation of forest fires due to soil dryness and vegetation.



What Can We Do to Help?


Regulate Water Consumption

It's crucial for citizens to regulate their water consumption to address drought in Catalonia. Reducing daily water usage through simple actions and adopting efficient technologies at home can make a big difference in conserving this vital resource. Awareness and education about the importance of water conservation, along with policies and regulations that promote responsible consumption practices, are essential to effectively address water scarcity and ensure a sustainable future for all.

Temporary Limitations

Regarding new tourist, industrial, service, commercial activities, and new urban planning, the Catalan Water Agency will have to assess the compatibility of the project or activity with the restrictions planned in the emergency phase. If it is incompatible, it cannot be implemented until the emergency phase is lifted. In the case of livestock farms in territories that are in an emergency phase, the entry of animals to new facilities will not be authorized, nor will the expansion of existing ones be allowed until the availability of water is ensured to guarantee animal welfare.
